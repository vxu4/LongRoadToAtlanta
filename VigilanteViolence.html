"I Killed a Jap"

"Oh, I killed a Jap” –Gunnar Lindberg after murdering Vietnamese American, Thien Minh Ly, Tustin California, 1999 Asians are the designated outsiders on America’s racial terrain. Whether cast as unassimilable "coolie" labor or as “White adjacent” model minorities, Asians are indelibly marked as aliens whose very presence threatens the well-being of “real” Americans. Much of the physical violence against various Asian and Pacific Islander people in the United States has been enacted by private citizens acting, individually and in groups, to rid the United States of this imagined menace. This map shows the pattern of vigilante racial violence that has followed Asian Americans into every corner of the United States from 1850 to the present.
