In the 19th Century, many states on the West Coast forbade Asians from testifying in courts or voting in local elections.  State and local governments across the country passed laws prohibiting Asians from marrying whites and, in some cases, other people of color.  State and local governments created taxes, fees, and regulations aimed at Asian businesses, and created segregated neighborhoods and schools for Asian children.  Sixteen states passed laws prohibiting Asian immigrants, from purchasing or leasing farmland.
